Pope John Paul II

Early life of Pope John Paul II

  • 1920 - Karol Józef Wojtyła was born in the Polish town of Wadowice, Poland
  • 1938 - Wojtyła and his father left Wadowice and moved to Kraków, where he enrolled at the Jagiellonian University
  • 1940 to 1944 - Wojtyła variously worked as a messenger for a restaurant, a manual labourer in a limestone quarry and for the Solvay chemical factory, in order to avoid deportation to Germany

"When Pope John Paul II kissed the ground at the Warsaw airport he began the process by which Communism in Poland — and ultimately elsewhere in Europe — would come to an end."
